Topic Map

Navigate the Act

Eight major clusters covering all key sections and rules of the Drugs & Cosmetics Act 1940.

ACT

Definitions & Key Terms

  • Drug: All medicines and substances for diagnosis, treatment, mitigation or prevention of disease [Sec 3(b)]
  • Cosmetic: Articles for cleansing, beautifying or altering appearance [Sec 3(aaa)]
  • Adulterated: Drug containing harmful or toxic substances [Sec 17A]
  • Spurious: Drug falsely claiming to be genuine [Sec 17B]
  • Misbranded: Drug with false, misleading or incomplete labelling [Sec 9A]
RULES

Licensing & Registration

  • Manufacturing License: Form 25/27 for allopathic drugs, granted by State SLA [Rules 69-70]
  • Retail License: Form 20/21 for chemists and druggists [Rule 65]
  • Import License: Form 8/8A from CDSCO [Rules 33-43]
  • Renewal Period: Manufacturing license valid for 5 years [Rule 70]
  • Licensing Authority: State authorities for manufacturing/retail, CDSCO for import [Sec 18]
RULES

Manufacturing Standards (GMP)

  • Mandatory Compliance: All manufacturers must comply with Schedule M GMP requirements [Rules 71, 74, 76]
  • Premises: Designed to prevent contamination, pest entry, cross-contamination [Schedule M, Part 1]
  • Equipment: Suitable for clean production with proper calibration and validation [Schedule M]
  • Personnel: Qualified pharmacist supervision mandatory [Rule 65]
  • Quality Systems: Documented procedures for manufacturing, testing, record-keeping [Schedule M]
ACT

Import & Export

  • Allowed Imports: Only drugs meeting Indian quality standards may be imported [Sec 10, Rule 33]
  • Prohibited Imports: Certain banned and dangerous drugs cannot be imported [Sec 10, Schedule E1]
  • License Required: Form 8/8A from CDSCO for all drug imports [Rules 33-43]
  • Customs Authority: Drugs inspected under Sea Customs Act. Form 6 filing required [Sec 11, 12]
  • Quality Certification: Must provide test reports and manufacturing data [Rule 35, 36]
RULES

Clinical Trials

  • Definition: Testing of new drugs in human subjects for safety and efficacy [Rule 122A-122N]
  • Approval Required: CDSCO must approve before trial commencement [Rules 122A, 122C]
  • Ethical Committee: Independent ethics committee must review and approve [Rule 122D]
  • Informed Consent: Subjects must provide written informed consent [Rule 122E]
  • New Drug Definition: A drug not yet sold in India [Rule 122E]
RULES

Cosmetics Regulations

  • Definition: Articles for cleansing, beautifying or altering appearance [Sec 3(aaa)]
  • Prohibited Substances: Cannot contain harmful chemicals, dyes, or preservatives [Sec 9C, 9D]
  • Labelling Rules: Must state composition, manufacturer, batch, expiry date [Rules 144-157]
  • Manufacturing: Form 28/29 license required. Schedule M GMP applies [Rules 144-157]
  • Penalties for Non-Compliance: Up to 1 year imprisonment or ₹20,000 fine [Sec 27A]
ACT

Ayurveda / Siddha / Unani

  • Recognition: Traditional systems recognized under the Act [Sec 3(a), 3A]
  • Manufacturing License: Form 24D for ASU drugs, granted by State SLA [Rule 150-169]
  • Authoritative Books: Drugs must match formulae in recognized classical texts [Sec 3(a)]
  • Quality Standards: Must comply with pharmacopeial standards [Schedule M]
  • Labelling: Must identify system (Ayurveda/Siddha/Unani) on packaging [Rules 150-169]
ACT

Penalties & Offences

  • Spurious Drug: 10 years to life imprisonment [Sec 27]
  • Adulterated Drug: Minimum 3 years imprisonment [Sec 27]
  • Sale Without License: 3 years imprisonment + fine [Sec 18]
  • Misbranded Drug: 1 year imprisonment + fine [Sec 28]
  • Obstruct Inspector: 3 years imprisonment [Sec 29]
Role Guides

Role-Specific Compliance

Specific licensing, obligations and restrictions for each stakeholder type.

⚠ Confirm requirements with your State Licensing Authority (SLA) before applying.

License Type & Form

Drug Manufacturing License Form 25 (Allopathic) / Form 24D (Ayurveda/Siddha/Unani) / Form 24F (Schedule X drugs) [Rules 69-70]

Licensing Authority

State Licensing Authority (SLA) Each state government appoints the authority responsible for granting manufacturing licenses [Sec 18, Rule 69]

Key Obligations

GMP Compliance (Mandatory) All manufacturing must follow Schedule M Good Manufacturing Practices. This includes premises design, equipment calibration, personnel qualification, quality control procedures, and documentation. [Schedule M, Rules 71, 74, 76]
Registered Pharmacist Supervision Manufacturing or dispensing must be under direct, personal supervision of a registered pharmacist. [Rule 65(1), (2)]
Labelling Rules All drugs must be properly labelled with name, composition, batch number, manufacturing date, expiry date, and manufacturer details. False or misleading labels are prohibited. [Rule 96]
Prohibition on Adulteration & Spurious Manufacture Manufacturing, selling or distributing adulterated or spurious drugs is a serious offence. No substandard or contaminated preparations allowed. [Sec 17A-17D, Sec 27]
Quality Standards All manufactured drugs must meet the standards of the Indian Pharmacopoeia or other recognized pharmacopeias. [Sec 8]
Record Keeping Maintain manufacturing records, batch records, quality control data, and raw material documentation for minimum 2 years. [Schedule M]

Prohibited Acts

  • Manufacturing without a valid license [Sec 18(c)]
  • Manufacturing adulterated drugs (harmful/toxic substances) [Sec 17A, 27]
  • Manufacturing spurious drugs (false identity/composition) [Sec 17B, 27]
  • Manufacturing misbranded drugs (false/misleading labels) [Sec 9A, 28]
  • Non-compliance with GMP [Schedule M]
  • Using banned drugs or ingredients [Sec 10, Schedule E1]

License Validity & Renewal

Validity: 5 years from date of grant. Renewal: 6 months before expiry. Renewal fee structure specified in Rules. [Rule 70]
⚠ Confirm requirements with CDSCO (Central authority) before importing any drugs.

License Type & Form

Import License or No Objection Certificate (NOC) Form 8 (Standard) / Form 8A (Specific Batches) [Rules 33-43]

Licensing Authority

CDSCO (Central Drugs Standards Control Organization) Federal authority responsible for drug import licensing and customs coordination. [Sec 10, Rules 33-43]

Key Obligations

Quality Standard Compliance All imported drugs must meet Indian quality standards as prescribed under the Indian Pharmacopoeia or equivalent. [Sec 8]
Prohibited Drug Check Cannot import drugs listed in the banned drug schedule (Schedule E1) or those specifically prohibited by CDSCO notification. [Sec 10, Schedule E1]
Sea Customs Act Compliance All drug imports subject to customs inspection, sea freight regulations, and Form 6 filing. [Sec 11, 12]
Test Reports & Certificates Must provide analytical reports from recognized laboratories, manufacturing authorization certificate, and quality certificates. [Rules 35, 36]
Labelling Compliance Imported drugs must have proper labelling with all required information before distribution. [Rule 96]

Prohibited Acts

  • Importing without valid license/NOC [Sec 10]
  • Importing banned or prohibited drugs [Sec 10, Schedule E1]
  • Importing drugs not meeting quality standards [Sec 8]
  • Importing adulterated or spurious drugs [Sec 27]
  • False declarations to customs [Sea Customs Act]

Process Overview

  1. Obtain CDSCO import license (Form 8/8A) [Rules 33-43]
  2. Prepare import documents (manufacturing authorization, test reports) [Rules 35-36]
  3. File Form 6 with customs authority [Sec 12]
  4. Arrange inspection by authorized officer [Sec 21-23]
  5. Obtain clearance and release certification [Sec 11]
⚠ Confirm requirements with your State Licensing Authority (SLA) before applying.

License Type & Form

Retail Drug License Form 20 (General) / Form 20A (Limited) / Form 21 (Cosmetics) [Rules 64-65]

Licensing Authority

State Licensing Authority (SLA) Each state government appoints the authority responsible for granting retail pharmacy licenses. [Sec 18, Rule 65]

Key Obligations

Registered Pharmacist Supervision All dispensing must be under direct, personal supervision of a registered pharmacist. Compounding must be by or under supervision of registered pharmacist. [Rule 65(1), (2)]
Schedule H Prescription Requirement Drugs specified in Schedule H (antibiotics, NSAIDs, corticosteroids, antihistamines) can ONLY be sold on valid medical prescription. Prescription must be recorded in a register. [Rule 65(3)]
Schedule H1 Prescription & Record Keeping Schedule H1 drugs (benzodiazepines, anticonvulsants) require prescription AND separate register records. Records must be maintained for 3 years. [Rule 65(3)(h)]
Schedule X Special Register Narcotic and psychotropic drugs (Schedule X) require special register entry with prescriber details, patient name, quantity, batch number. [Rule 65(3)]
Prescription Register Maintenance All prescriptions for Schedule H drugs must be recorded with: date, prescriber name/address, patient name/address, drug name/quantity, batch number, expiry date. Records kept minimum 2 years. [Rule 65(3)(1)]
Cash/Credit Memo Documentation For general retail sales, must issue cash/credit memo with pharmacy name, drug name, quantity, date. [Rule 65(4)(3)]
Labelling & Batch Information Cannot sell drugs without proper labels. Must ensure batch number and expiry date visible to customer. [Rule 96]

Prohibited Acts

  • Selling without valid retail license [Sec 18(c)]
  • Selling Schedule H drugs without prescription [Rule 65(3)]
  • Selling Schedule H1 drugs without proper records [Rule 65(3)(h)]
  • Selling expired drugs [Sec 9A]
  • Selling adulterated or spurious drugs [Sec 27]
  • Selling without pharmacist supervision [Rule 65(2)]
  • Failing to maintain prescription records [Rule 65(3)]

License Validity & Renewal

Validity: 3–5 years (varies by state). Renewal: Apply before expiry. Maintain compliance with all conditions. [Rule 65]

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about drugs, cosmetics, licensing, and compliance under the Act.

General
Q: What is a "new drug" under the Act? +
A: A "new drug" is a drug that has not been sold in India as a prescription or over-the-counter medication. It includes drugs with a new chemical entity, new indication, new dosage form, or new combination not previously used in India. Before manufacture or clinical trials, CDSCO approval must be obtained. [Rule 122E]
Q: What is the difference between spurious and adulterated drugs? +
A: Spurious drug: A drug falsely claiming to be of a particular drug or manufacturer. Adulterated drug: A drug containing harmful, extraneous, or impure substances. Penalties for spurious (7-life years) are harsher than adulterated (3-5 years). [Sec 17A, 17B, 27]
Q: Who is CDSCO and what do they control? +
A: CDSCO (Central Drugs Standards Control Organization) is the federal drug regulatory authority under the Ministry of Health. They grant drug import licenses, approve new drugs for clinical trials, establish pharmacopeial standards, and oversee inter-state drug distribution. State authorities handle manufacturing and retail licensing. [Sec 2, 10, 18, Rules 33-43]
Manufacturer
Q: Can I manufacture drugs without GMP compliance? +
A: No. GMP compliance is MANDATORY for all pharmaceutical manufacturers. Failure to comply will result in license cancellation and criminal penalties including imprisonment. [Schedule M, Rules 71, 74, 76]
Q: Who approves a new drug for manufacture in India? +
A: For a new drug (not yet sold in India), CDSCO must grant approval after review of clinical data, safety, and efficacy. Without CDSCO approval, manufacture is illegal. Approval takes 90-180 days typically. [Rule 122A-122N]
Q: What happens if my manufacturing license lapses? +
A: Once your license expires, you CANNOT continue manufacturing. If you manufacture after expiry, you can be prosecuted for manufacturing without a license (3 years imprisonment + fine). Apply for renewal before expiry. [Sec 18, Rule 70]
Q: Which document specifies GMP standards for my facility? +
A: Schedule M of the Drugs & Cosmetics Rules specifies all GMP requirements covering premises design, equipment, personnel, materials, manufacturing processes, and quality control. [Schedule M, Rules 71, 74, 76]
Retailer / Chemist
Q: Can I sell a Schedule H drug without prescription? +
A: NO. Schedule H drugs can ONLY be sold on a valid prescription from a registered medical practitioner. Selling without prescription is a serious violation (1 year imprisonment + ₹20,000 fine). [Rule 65(3)]
Q: How long must I keep prescription records? +
A: Schedule H drug records: minimum 2 years. Schedule H1 records: 3 years. These must be available for inspection by the licensing authority. [Rule 65(3), Rule 65(3)(h)]
Q: What is the difference between Schedule H and Schedule H1 drugs? +
A: Schedule H: Large category of prescription drugs (antibiotics, NSAIDs, antihistamines). Schedule H1: Restricted category (benzodiazepines, anticonvulsants) with stricter controls, separate register, and 3-year records. [Rule 65(3), Rule 65(3)(h)]
Q: What extra rules apply to Schedule X drugs? +
A: Schedule X (narcotics and psychotropics) require: special register entry, prescriber details recorded, patient identification, batch/expiry recorded, records kept indefinitely for inspection. Diversion or loss must be reported. [Rule 65(3)]
Importer
Q: Which drugs are banned from import? +
A: Schedule E1 lists all banned drugs that cannot be imported under any circumstances. Any attempt results in seizure, criminal prosecution (3 years imprisonment + fine), and forfeiture. [Sec 10, Schedule E1]
Q: Can I import a banned drug with special permission? +
A: No. Banned drugs cannot be imported under ANY circumstances. The only exception is authorized research institutions with CDSCO written approval for specific clinical/research purposes. [Sec 10, Schedule E1]
Q: What customs rules apply to drug imports? +
A: Drug imports are regulated under the Sea Customs Act in addition to the Drugs & Cosmetics Act. All shipments must be declared (Form 6), inspected by customs, released only after drug inspector clearance. False declarations are a criminal offense. [Sec 11, 12, Sea Customs Act]
Q: Which authority grants import licenses? +
A: CDSCO grants all drug import licenses and NOCs. Applications in Form 8 (standard) or Form 8A (specific imported batches). Approval takes 6-8 weeks. State authorities do not grant import licenses. [Rules 33-43]
